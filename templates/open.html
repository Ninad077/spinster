About Spinster

A UI for readers by a reader.

What the hell is Spinster?

Spinster is a website that talks about novels—good or bad. The idea is to give readers an overview of how I felt about the novel (without spoilers, of course!). For me, it’s like a scribble pad where I pen down my thoughts with honesty about the books I read. It’s a medium to express myself and vent my feelings—whether stark or mild—about something I experienced, which, as a corporate slave, one rarely gets to do. So yeah, if you’re into books and love talking about them, feel free to explore this website. Have fun—that’s the objective.

What can Spinster do for me?

Absolutely nothing. The only purpose is to make a reader aware of my perspective about a book. I know it sounds very selfish, but sadly can't help it. It is what it is. Think of it as a bird’s-eye view that helps readers decide whether they’d want to dive into the book or not. Again, just sharing my take, but I’d love to know if you saw the book differently. The website has Book reviews divided into plot summary, Ratings, What I liked & disliked about the book and a small snippet about the Author it belongs to. So yeah, go ahead, browse through the website, and feel free to share your thoughts—good or bad, I’d love to hear them.

The Silent Patient

by Alex Michaelides

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
3.5/5.0
Psychological Thriller Read in 2025 352 Pages

Plot Summary

Alicia Berenson's life is seemingly perfect. A famous painter married to an in-demand fashion photographer, she lives in a grand house overlooking a park in one of London's most desirable areas. One evening her husband Gabriel returns home late from a fashion shoot, and Alicia shoots him five times in the face, and then never speaks another word. The price of her art skyrockets, and she, the silent patient, is hidden away from the tabloids and spotlight at the Grove, a secure forensic unit in North London.

What I Loved

What truly captivated me about this novel was the psychological depth and complexity of the narrative that kept me thoroughly engaged throughout. The brilliant plot twist genuinely caught me off guard, which is rare in psychological thrillers these days. I was particularly impressed by the beautiful integration of Greek mythology with modern psychology, creating layers of meaning that enriched the reading experience. The compelling unreliable narrator kept me questioning everything I thought I knew, making every revelation feel earned and impactful. The atmospheric writing style created genuine tension that had me turning pages well into the night.

What I Disliked

While I enjoyed the book overall, there were some aspects that didn't quite work for me. The pacing felt uneven, particularly in the middle section where the story seemed to drag a bit before picking up momentum again. Some of the coincidences that drove the plot forward felt a bit too convenient for my liking, which occasionally pulled me out of the story. I found myself wishing for more development of the supporting characters, as they sometimes felt more like plot devices than fully realized people. Additionally, while the ending was shocking and well-executed, I felt it could have been foreshadowed more skillfully to make the revelation feel more organic to the story.

Alex Michaelides

British-Cypriot author and screenwriter, known for psychological thrillers. Former psychotherapist turned novelist, bringing deep understanding of human psychology to his gripping narratives.

Verity

by Colleen Hoover

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
3.5/5.0
Psychological Thriller Read in 2025 336 Pages

Plot Summary

Verity by Colleen Hoover is a dark psychological thriller about Lowen Ashleigh, a struggling writer who is hired to finish the works of bestselling author Verity Crawford after an accident leaves her unable to continue. While staying in Verity’s home, Lowen uncovers disturbing secrets hidden among Verity’s notes, blurring the lines between truth and fiction. The story unfolds with mounting tension, forbidden attraction, and a chilling atmosphere that keeps readers questioning whats real until the very end.

What I Loved

The most attractive thing about Verity is the fact that the book is very fast paced and it keeps you intrigued about the story till the end. I really loved the dynamic of Lowen with Jeremy and how it evolves over time. I loved how the author made us feel intimidated by Verity and equally empathise with her at the same time, which is a paradox I rarely feel about a book. This book is a treat for all those who would read it for the first time. It is very dark, gritty and keeps you up on the edge.

What I Disliked

Despite all the brownie points this book has, the end twist is somewhat implausible. There is an element of disbelief that we as readers constantly feel. At times, the book goes a little more descriptive about passion and goes a little too literal complimenting male physique (one comprehends this since it is written by a woman) but being a guy I think I could have done without it. Apart from that, I feel the author could have cut down little about the backstory for Lowen and could have explored Verity more which could have made the reader feel the agony a lot more.

Colleen Hoover

Colleen Hoover is a master of contemporary romance and emotional storytelling, known for her heart-wrenching plots and unforgettable characters.

And Then There were None

by Agatha Christie

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
4.0/5.0
Murder Mystery Read in 2025 336 Pages

Plot Summary

Ten strangers are invited to a secluded island mansion off the Devon coast. Cut off from the mainland, with their mysterious host nowhere to be found, they soon realize that something sinister is at play. As fear grips the guests, each must confront the shadows of their past in this haunting and masterful tale of suspense.

What I Loved

The arrangement of Ten unknown people getting trapped on a Unknow Island with nowhere to go! I loved the premise! This book was electrifying from start to end. The spin of the poem "Ten little soldiers" coupled with the shocking incidents happening inside the house was so fascinating to read. I am not going to lie, but I was biting my nails and was equally pumped to see what would happen next. The apprehension of guests, the cryptic hosts, the sinister air of island and a killer admist, a combination a reader craves to read. I loved this book. A solid 4 for me!

What I Disliked

When a book is so good, one often tends to ignore the negative aspect of it. However, I won't. The plot when its releaved, seems half convincing and feels like the author wants the reader to blindly believe the end. I, on the other hand, felt like she could have made it sound more believable. Rest assured, the book is a one fun ride.

Agatha Christie

The Murder of Roger Ackroyd

by Agatha Christie

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
5.0/5.0
Murder Mystery Read in 2025 368 Pages

Plot Summary

In the quiet English village of King’s Abbot, the sudden death of a wealthy widow shocks the community. Soon after, the prosperous Roger Ackroyd is found murdered in his country home under mysterious circumstances. The case seems tangled with secrets, half-truths, and hidden motives, leaving everyone around him under suspicion. Into this unsettling situation steps Hercule Poirot, the brilliant Belgian detective who has retired to the countryside. Drawn reluctantly back into investigation, he must untangle a web of lies and uncover the truth behind a crime that defies easy answers.

What I Loved

This is like a perfect book for me. It is so perfect that it has ruined murder mysteries for me, since I subconciously compare them with this book. I could sing accolades about this book and still won't be enough. The theatrical setting of the book, the cogent interrogation of the suspects by Poirot, everything is just impeccable. It is not a page turner and neither very fast paced. It is designed to be laid back and let readers keep guessing from the usual suspects. The cherry on the cake for me is the narration of the book which is unlikely for a Christie book. One of a kind truly. If the end of this book won't shock you, I would change my name rightaway! The mystery about Ralph Paton, the anonymous letter to Mrs. Ferrars, coupled with the malign family realtions make this a compelling read. Loveeeeeed it.

What I Disliked

This might be the first time I am writing this, but nothing. The Book is Flawless.

Murder on the Orient Express

by Agatha Christie

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
2.0/5.0
Murder Mystery Read in 2025 368 Pages

Plot Summary

On a winter journey aboard the luxurious Orient Express, an American passenger named Samuel Ratchett is found murdered in his compartment. With the train halted by snow, detective Hercule Poirot investigates the case, uncovering tangled secrets, hidden connections, and a complex web of motives among the passengers.

What I Loved

There are some books I feel have a good premise and could have done a lot better and this book is certainly one of them. A murder taking place in a train, with multiple suspects, a snowstorm outside was a good premise to begin with. The only plus thing for me in this book is the way Poirot investigates each and every passenger. Smooth like butter. I love myself some Poirot every now and then.

What I Disliked

As you might have noticed from the rating. This book is beyond my comprehension. The plot is incredulous and the narration seem to be intentionally weaved in a way to corroborate the plot. It was hard for me to give in to the author's sensibilities this time. Christie is my favorite, but this one for me was the least favorite creation

Evil Under the Sun

by Agatha Christie

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
4.0/5.0
Murder Mystery Read in 2025 368 Pages

Plot Summary

At a glamorous seaside resort, the stunning Arlena Stuart’s beauty and flirtations stir envy, tension, and desire among the other guests. When a shocking crime shatters the holiday calm, Hercule Poirot must untangle hidden rivalries, secret motives, and carefully guarded truths to reveal what lies beneath the bright summer sunshine.

What I Loved

I loved this book. Period. It is such a wholesome book. It has the traditional central character like Arlena who thrives on scandals and then we have her devoted husband, a stepdaugher who hates her & a beau who is infatuated by her. The story weaves around the murder of Arlena with a needle pointing to every guests present in the hotel. The fun thing is how the story just flows and how several incidents keep on happening. I was so engrossed reading this book and was so enjoying the drama that I almost forgot to predict the traditional whodunit. The end was so satisfying and believable and by the end of the book we do root for the characters in the story which is a testament of how beautiful the writing has been. I loved this book.

What I Disliked

I always expect something mind-boggling from Agatha Christie. The reveal was shocking but not utterly baffling. Nevertheless, its a pretty book to devour in one sitting. A feast for the readers, for sure!
